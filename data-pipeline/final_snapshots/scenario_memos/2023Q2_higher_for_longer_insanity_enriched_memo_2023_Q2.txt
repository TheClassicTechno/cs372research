## Macro Environment Overview

[L0-MACRO-REGIME] As of the end of Q2 2023, financial markets are attempting to interpret a complex macro environment characterized by persistent inflation, resilient economic growth, and rapidly rising interest rates.

[L0-INFLATION-PERSISTENCE] Inflation has moderated from its 2022 peak but remains above the Federal Reserve’s long-run target, raising the possibility that policy rates may remain elevated for an extended period.

[L0-GROWTH-RESILIENCE] Economic activity and labor markets have remained more resilient than many forecasters expected despite aggressive monetary tightening during 2022–2023.

[L0-RATE-SHOCK] Long-term Treasury yields and real interest rates have increased significantly over the past year, tightening financial conditions and raising discount rates applied to future corporate earnings.

[L0-AI-RALLY] Equity markets have nevertheless rallied during 2023, led primarily by large-cap technology companies benefiting from strong investor enthusiasm surrounding artificial intelligence.

These conditions create several competing interpretations of the current macroeconomic regime.

## Competing Macro Theses

The following interpretations represent plausible explanations for current market behavior.

Agents should evaluate which interpretation is most consistent with the available evidence.

### Thesis A — Higher-for-Longer Interest Rates

[L0-INFLATION-PERSISTENCE] Persistent inflation may require central banks to maintain elevated policy rates for longer than markets previously expected.

[L0-RATE-SHOCK] Higher real interest rates increase discount rates applied to future earnings, placing structural pressure on long-duration growth assets.

[L0-CASHFLOW-PREMIUM] Under this interpretation, sectors with strong current cash flows and inflation sensitivity may outperform more duration-sensitive sectors.

### Thesis B — Growth Resilience

[L0-GROWTH-RESILIENCE] Economic activity has remained stronger than expected despite tighter monetary policy and rising borrowing costs.

[L0-CYCLICAL-DEMAND] If growth continues to exceed expectations, corporate earnings may remain robust across cyclical sectors.

[L0-RISK-APPETITE] Under this interpretation, markets may sustain risk-taking behavior despite higher interest rates.

### Thesis C — AI Productivity Boom

[L0-AI-RALLY] Rapid advances in artificial intelligence have generated strong investor demand for semiconductor and cloud infrastructure companies.

[L0-AI-PRODUCTIVITY] If AI adoption produces meaningful productivity gains across the economy, technology earnings growth may justify elevated valuations even in a higher-rate environment.

[L0-TECH-LEADERSHIP] Under this interpretation, technology leadership could persist despite macro headwinds.

### Thesis D — Delayed Recession Risk

[L0-POLICY-LAGS] Monetary policy operates with long and variable lags, meaning the full economic impact of higher interest rates may not yet be reflected in current economic data.

[L0-DEMAND-SLOWDOWN] If tighter financial conditions eventually slow consumption and investment, economic growth could weaken in coming quarters.

[L0-DEFENSIVE-ROTATION] Under this interpretation, defensive sectors may outperform if economic conditions deteriorate.

Agents may adopt, reject, or combine these interpretations when forming their investment thesis.
These interpretations are not mutually exclusive, and multiple mechanisms may be operating simultaneously in the current macro environment.

LAYER 0 — MACRO NARRATIVE CONTEXT

The following statements summarize key developments during the
post-pandemic economic recovery leading into Q3 2021.

These statements represent **interpretive context**, not definitive
causal conclusions.

Agents may cite these statements using the bracketed evidence IDs.

[L0-STIMULUS]
Large fiscal stimulus programs during 2020–2021 injected substantial
liquidity into the economy, boosting household balance sheets and
consumer spending capacity.

[L0-SUPPLY]
Global supply chains remain disrupted following pandemic lockdowns,
with shipping congestion, semiconductor shortages, and logistics
constraints affecting multiple industries.

[L0-REOPEN]
Economic reopening following COVID lockdowns has produced rapid
demand recovery across travel, goods, and services sectors.

[L0-LABOR]
Labor markets have tightened in several sectors during the reopening
phase of the economy, with firms reporting hiring difficulties and
rising wage pressures.

[L0-INFL-DEBATE]
Policymakers and market participants remain divided on whether the
recent rise in inflation reflects **temporary reopening effects**
or a more **persistent structural inflation regime**.

[L0-FED-STANCE]
The Federal Reserve has maintained near-zero interest rates and
continued asset purchases throughout most of 2021, emphasizing
patience before tightening policy.

[L0-REALRATES]
Real interest rates remain deeply negative, reflecting accommodative
monetary policy and strong demand for safe assets.

[L0-ENERGY_CYCLE]
Energy markets tightened during 2021 as global demand recovered
faster than supply capacity, contributing to rising oil prices.

[L0-EQUITY_LEADERSHIP]
Large-cap technology stocks continued to dominate equity market
performance during the post-pandemic recovery period.

[L0-VOL_SHIFT]
Market volatility increased periodically during 2021 as investors
reassessed inflation risks and potential monetary tightening.

MACRO REGIME UNCERTAINTY MAP

The current macro environment may be consistent with **multiple
competing interpretations**.

Agents should treat these regimes as **competing hypotheses** rather
than established truths.

Agents are encouraged to consider the probability of each regime
when constructing portfolio allocations.

[L0-R1] Transitory Inflation Reopening
Inflation reflects temporary reopening dynamics and supply constraints.
As supply chains normalize, inflation pressures may ease without
aggressive monetary tightening.

[L0-R2] Persistent Inflation Regime
Strong fiscal stimulus and tightening labor markets may sustain
inflation above target for an extended period.

[L0-R3] Policy Tightening Risk
If inflation remains elevated, central banks may tighten policy faster
than markets currently expect, potentially slowing economic growth.

[L0-R4] Continued Liquidity Expansion
Monetary policy may remain accommodative longer than expected,
supporting risk assets despite inflation pressures.

[L0-R5] Commodity Cycle Upswing
Energy and commodity markets may be entering a cyclical upswing due
to recovering global demand and constrained supply.

Agents should evaluate which regimes are most plausible and how
different regimes would affect sector and asset performance.

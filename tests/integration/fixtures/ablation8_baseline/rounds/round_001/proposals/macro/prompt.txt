Rules:

• Every claim must affect at least one ticker in the allocation universe
• Every ticker with weight > 0 must appear in at least one claim’s `"impacts_positions"`
• `"impacts_positions"` should include only tickers from the allocation universe

This prevents dead reasoning that does not affect the portfolio.

## POSITION RATIONALE RULE

Every ticker with weight > 0 MUST appear in `"position_rationale"` and MUST reference **at least one claim ID**.

Example:

{
  "ticker": "NVDA",
  "weight": 0.30,
  "supported_by_claims": ["C2", "C3"],
  "explanation": "C2 supports tech leadership while C3 supports firm-specific upside."
}

Rules:

• Every ticker with weight > 0 must have a rationale entry
• The `"weight"` in each rationale entry must match the `"allocation"` object
• `"supported_by_claims"` must reference valid claim IDs
• If a ticker has weight 0.0, it does not need a position_rationale entry

This creates a **traceable mapping between reasoning and allocation decisions**.

## PORTFOLIO COHERENCE RULE

Your portfolio must be consistent with your claims.

That means:

• if a claim says a sector or factor should outperform, the allocation should reflect that
• if a claim identifies major risk, the allocation should reflect reduced exposure where appropriate
• do NOT produce claims that are disconnected from the final weights
• do NOT produce weights that are not justified by claims

Unsupported allocations will be treated as weak reasoning.

---

## ALLOCATION RULES

• Include EVERY ticker from the allocation universe — no more, no fewer
• Weights must be between **0.0 and 1.00** (max 100% per ticker)
• Weights MUST **sum to 1.0**
• At least **1 tickers must have weight > 0**
• A weight of **0.0 means zero allocation**

Higher weight = stronger conviction.

## CLAIM RULES

• Use **3–5 claims maximum**

• Each claim must have a **unique ID**
 C1, C2, C3, ...

• Each claim must represent a **distinct analytical hypothesis**

• Claims should follow a structure appropriate to your analytical framework

• Each claim should represent a distinct mechanism relevant to your decision lens

• Claims should be **concise and atomic**

• Do NOT write long essays or bundle multiple unrelated ideas into one claim

• Claims that do not influence any positions are INVALID

## CLAIM TYPE RULE

Every claim MUST include a `"claim_type"` field:

• `"macro"` — macro regime, rates, inflation, economic environment
• `"sector"` — sector/industry dynamics, factor exposure
• `"firm"` — company-specific fundamentals, filings, earnings
• `"risk"` — volatility, correlation, drawdown, portfolio risk dynamics
• `"technical"` — price action, momentum, trend behavior

`claim_type` is REQUIRED for each claim.

Every claim MUST include a `"reasoning_type"` field:

• `"causal"` — if X changes, Y will follow (intervention or counterfactual reasoning)
• `"observational"` — X is associated with Y (correlation or pattern)
• `"risk_assessment"` — X creates vulnerability to Y (conditional risk)
• `"pattern"` — price behavior suggests continuation or reversal

## CLAIM EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT

Every claim MUST cite **at least one valid evidence ID**.

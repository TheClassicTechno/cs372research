## Critique of Other Agents

You must critique at least one other agent per round. Your critique should:

1. Identify the weakest assumption in their thesis
2. Explain the specific mechanism by which that assumption could fail
3. Describe the portfolio consequences if their assumption fails

Target your critiques at these specific vulnerabilities:

Against the Inflation Persistence Strategist:
- They are extrapolating peak inflation forward indefinitely. This is
  textbook anchoring bias. EVERY inflationary episode in the last 50
  years has been followed by disinflation. The question is when, not if.
- Energy supply constraints are real but the PRICE already reflects them.
  At $100+ oil, XOM is priced for perfection. Any demand destruction
  creates enormous downside risk.
- Their thesis ignores substitution effects. High energy prices accelerate
  the energy transition, increase efficiency investment, and ultimately
  destroy the demand that supports their thesis.
- Buying energy at cycle highs because "this time it's structural" is
  the same mistake people made with tech in 2000.

Against the Recession Strategist:
- They have been calling for recession for months while the economy
  continues to grow. At what point is a persistently wrong forecast
  simply wrong?
- Defensive positioning is a GUARANTEED drag on returns if recession
  does not materialize (or is mild). The opportunity cost of hiding
  in WMT and JNJ while tech rebounds is enormous.
- They treat yield curve inversion as gospel but ignore the variable
  and often extended lead time. Selling tech in response to a signal
  that has a 12-18 month lead time is premature.
- Tech earnings are more resilient than they assume. Cloud revenue
  is contractual and sticky. Digital advertising recovers quickly.

Against the Soft Landing Strategist:
- They are directionally right but positioned in the wrong assets.
  If the economy avoids recession, tech benefits MORE than cyclicals
  because tech has already de-rated while cyclicals have not.
- Financials are not a clean economic play — they carry credit risk,
  regulatory risk, and trading revenue volatility. Tech is a purer
  way to express the "no recession" thesis.
- Cyclicals give you economic beta. Tech gives you economic beta
  PLUS secular growth. The latter dominates over any 12+ month horizon.

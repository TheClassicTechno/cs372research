## Revision Tone: ADVERSARIAL — FIRM

Your objective is to **stress-test your own thesis under critique and
defend it only if it survives rigorous scrutiny.**

Treat critiques as deliberate attempts to break your argument.

Your task is to determine whether the critiques expose:

• a flawed assumption
• an incorrect causal mechanism
• insufficient evidence for a claim
• an allocation that is stronger than the reasoning justifies

Do not concede points lightly, but do not defend reasoning that cannot
be logically supported.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Core Revision Principle

A strong adversarial revision must demonstrate that your thesis either:

A) **survives critique with stronger reasoning**, or
B) **requires a targeted correction to a specific assumption**

The goal is **intellectual robustness**, not stubborn defense.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Adversarial Revision Rules

When evaluating critiques:

• Accept a critique **ONLY if it reveals a genuine logical failure** in
  your reasoning.

Examples of valid failures include:

- a causal mechanism that does not follow from the evidence
- a key assumption contradicted by memo evidence
- a portfolio allocation that exceeds what the reasoning supports

Reject critiques that merely:

- propose alternative interpretations without invalidating your thesis
- rely on speculation rather than evidence
- challenge peripheral aspects that do not affect the core argument


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Required Reasoning Actions

Perform the following reasoning steps:

1. **Identify the core assumption of your thesis**

   Determine the single most important condition that must hold for
   your thesis to remain valid.

2. **Evaluate whether critiques break this assumption**

   Determine whether the critiques:

   • directly contradict the assumption
   • weaken the causal chain supporting the thesis
   • reduce confidence in the expected outcome

3. **Defend or repair the thesis**

   If the thesis survives:

   • strengthen the causal explanation
   • clarify assumptions
   • reinforce evidential support

   If a critique exposes a real weakness:

   • revise the specific assumption that failed
   • repair the causal chain accordingly

4. **Reassess the allocation**

   Determine whether the critique affects:

   • the validity of the thesis
   • the confidence of the thesis
   • the appropriate portfolio weight


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Acceptance Constraint

You should accept **at most ONE major critique** unless multiple critiques
invalidate the same core assumption.

This ensures that revisions remain **focused and logically coherent**.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Portfolio Revision Principles

Your portfolio should remain consistent with your defended thesis.

Apply the following rules:

• If the thesis remains intact, maintain the overall allocation strategy.

• If a core assumption weakens but does not fail, reduce exposure
  proportionally.

• If a core assumption fails, revise the thesis and adjust the
  allocation accordingly.

Avoid dramatic allocation swings unless the critique genuinely
invalidates the thesis.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Important Constraints

• Do not repeat your previous argument without strengthening its logic.

• Do not concede critiques that do not materially weaken your thesis.

• Do not defend reasoning that clearly contradicts memo evidence.

• Your revision must demonstrate **either stronger reasoning or a
  corrected assumption.**


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Required Structure

Core assumption
- the key condition that must hold for your thesis

Accepted critiques
- critiques that successfully challenge your reasoning

Rejected critiques
- critiques that fail to invalidate your thesis

Reasoning defense or repair
- explanation showing how your thesis survives or how it was corrected

Revised thesis
- clarified thesis with stronger causal reasoning

Portfolio changes
- allocation adjustments justified by the revised reasoning

## Revision Tone: ADVERSARIAL — FIRM

Your objective is to defend your thesis against critique and expose
weaknesses in opposing reasoning. Stand by your analysis firmly.

Treat critiques as attempts to break your argument.

You should assume critiques may contain:
• flawed assumptions
• incomplete causal reasoning
• selective use of evidence

Revision rules:

• Accept a critique ONLY if it exposes a clear logical contradiction,
  incorrect causal mechanism, or unsupported claim in your thesis.

• Reject critiques that merely propose alternative interpretations,
  different weightings of evidence, or speculative counter-scenarios.

• You should accept at most ONE critique unless multiple critiques
  invalidate the same core assumption.

Reasoning update requirement:

You MUST either:
A) strengthen your existing thesis with clearer causal reasoning
or
B) revise a specific assumption that was successfully challenged.

Do NOT repeat your previous argument verbatim.

Portfolio revision rules:

• Maintain your allocation strategy unless a critique invalidates a
  core premise of your thesis.

• If revisions occur, adjust allocations modestly rather than
  restructuring the portfolio.

• Avoid large allocation swings unless your core thesis changes.

Required structure:

Accepted critiques:
- list critiques you accept and why

Rejected critiques:
- list critiques you reject and why

Revised thesis:
- restate your thesis with improved causal reasoning

Portfolio changes:
- describe any allocation adjustments and why

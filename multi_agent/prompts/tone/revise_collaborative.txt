## Revision Tone: COLLABORATIVE — INTEGRATIVE

Your objective is to **improve the overall reasoning quality of the debate**
by integrating useful critiques and strengthening the explanatory framework.

Treat critiques from other agents as **candidate improvements to your thesis**.

Your goal is not to defend your original argument, but to produce a **more
rigorous and comprehensive explanation of the situation**.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Core Revision Principle

A strong revision should demonstrate **clear reasoning improvement**.

This may include:

• clarifying causal mechanisms
• incorporating additional evidence
• addressing previously ignored risks or counterforces
• adjusting portfolio allocations to better match the reasoning


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Role Consistency Requirement

You must maintain your **analytical lens as a {{ role }} agent**.

Do not adopt another agent’s reasoning framework.

Instead:

• interpret critiques through your own analytical perspective
• integrate insights that improve your thesis without abandoning your role


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Reasoning Update Requirements

During revision, perform the following steps:

1. **Evaluate critiques**

   Determine which critiques materially improve the analysis.

   Accept critiques that provide:

   • stronger evidence
   • clearer causal mechanisms
   • identification of overlooked risks
   • improved explanations of market dynamics

2. **Repair reasoning where necessary**

   If critiques expose weaknesses in your thesis:

   • clarify ambiguous causal steps
   • strengthen evidential grounding
   • refine assumptions
   • incorporate missing mechanisms

3. **Integrate useful insights**

   When appropriate, combine insights from multiple agents into a
   more comprehensive explanation.

4. **Update thesis confidence**

   Determine whether critiques should:

   • strengthen your conviction
   • weaken your conviction
   • shift the emphasis of the thesis


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Portfolio Revision Principles

Portfolio adjustments should follow from the revised reasoning.

When updating allocations:

• ensure position sizes reflect the **strength of the evidence**

• reduce exposure if the thesis became less certain

• increase exposure if the reasoning became more robust

• avoid mechanical adjustments that are not supported by reasoning


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Important Constraints

• Do not ignore critiques that materially improve reasoning.

• Do not accept critiques that contradict strong evidence.

• Do not preserve your original allocation if the reasoning no longer
  justifies it.

• Avoid extreme portfolio swings unless the revised reasoning clearly
  supports a different thesis.

• Your revision should produce **a clearer and more coherent causal
  explanation than your original argument.**


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Required Structure

Accepted critiques
- insights from other agents that materially improved your reasoning

Rejected critiques
- critiques that remain unsupported or inconsistent with evidence

Reasoning updates
- how your causal explanation or assumptions changed

Revised thesis
- improved explanation incorporating the strongest insights

Portfolio changes
- allocation updates justified by the revised reasoning

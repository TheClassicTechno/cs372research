## Debate Tone: ADVERSARIAL

Your objective is to aggressively stress-test the other agent's thesis.

Assume their reasoning may contain hidden assumptions, incorrect causal
mechanisms, or unsupported conclusions.

Your role is to expose those weaknesses.

Focus on identifying:

• incorrect or incomplete causal mechanisms
• unsupported claims or missing evidence
• contradictions between reasoning and portfolio allocations
• ignored counter-evidence in the memo
• fragile assumptions that break under plausible scenarios

Attack the *core logic* of the argument rather than minor stylistic issues.

Avoid nitpicking wording or superficial disagreements.

Critique rules:

• Target the most important claims in the argument.

• For each critique, reference the specific claim or assumption you
  are challenging.

• Whenever possible, cite counter-evidence from the memo.

• Focus on whether the thesis logically supports the portfolio allocation.

Do NOT soften critiques to reach agreement.

If the argument relies on a weak causal mechanism, challenge it directly.

Required structure:

Major causal weaknesses:
- identify mechanisms that do not logically support the conclusion

Unsupported or weakly supported claims:
- claims lacking sufficient evidence or relying on speculation

Contradictions or inconsistencies:
- reasoning that conflicts with the portfolio allocation or other claims

Alternative interpretation:
- briefly explain how the same evidence could support a different thesis

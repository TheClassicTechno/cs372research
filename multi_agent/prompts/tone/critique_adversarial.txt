## Debate Tone: ADVERSARIAL

Your objective is to **actively attempt to falsify the other agent’s thesis.**

Treat the argument as a hypothesis that must survive rigorous scrutiny.

Assume the reasoning may contain:

• hidden assumptions
• incorrect causal mechanisms
• overconfident conclusions
• missing counter-evidence
• allocations that exceed what the evidence justifies

Your task is to **identify the weakest structural point in the argument and show why it may fail.**

Focus on breaking the argument at its most important link.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Adversarial Evaluation Principles

Prioritize critiques that would **materially invalidate the thesis or the portfolio allocation**.

Focus especially on:

• **causal chain failure**
  whether the evidence actually supports the mechanism being claimed

• **evidential gaps**
  whether key claims lack concrete support from the memo

• **assumption fragility**
  whether the argument depends on a small number of uncertain conditions

• **allocation overreach**
  whether the portfolio position size is stronger than the evidence warrants

• **ignored counterforces**
  whether important macro, sector, or risk dynamics were omitted


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Required Reasoning Actions

Your critique must include the following reasoning steps:

1. **Identify the critical assumption**

   Determine the single most important assumption required for the thesis
   to hold. This may involve macro conditions, behavioral assumptions,
   market structure, or causal relationships.

2. **Stress-test the causal chain**

   Explain where the logic connecting evidence → thesis → allocation may break.

   Ask:

   • Does the evidence actually imply the claimed mechanism?
   • Could the same evidence arise under a different explanation?

3. **Test allocation justification**

   Evaluate whether the portfolio weight is logically justified.

   Determine whether the evidence supports:

   • the thesis itself
   • the confidence of the thesis
   • the size of the position

4. **Construct a competing interpretation**

   Provide a strong alternative explanation of the same evidence.

   The alternative should be **plausible and coherent**, not a trivial objection.

5. **Identify a failure scenario**

   Describe a realistic scenario in which the thesis would fail and the
   portfolio allocation would perform poorly.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Evidence Use

Whenever possible:

• cite specific counter-evidence from the memo

• highlight when evidence supports a weaker claim than the agent asserts

• identify when key claims rely on speculation rather than data

• explain how omitted evidence could materially change the conclusion


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Important Constraints

• Focus on **high-impact weaknesses**, not minor imperfections.

• Do not waste effort on wording or stylistic issues.

• Do not artificially agree with arguments that are weak.

• If the argument is strong, you must still identify the **most plausible
  failure mode**.


---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Required Structure

Critical assumption
- the key condition that must hold for the thesis to work

Causal chain weaknesses
- where the reasoning linking evidence → conclusion may break

Allocation challenge
- whether the evidence truly justifies the position size

Counter-evidence
- memo evidence that conflicts with the thesis

Competing interpretation
- a coherent alternative explanation of the same evidence

Failure scenario
- a plausible situation where the thesis would fail

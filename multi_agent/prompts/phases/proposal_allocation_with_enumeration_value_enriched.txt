---SECTION: reasoning_structure---

Your reasoning must follow this structure.

First, produce **numbered claims**.

Each claim must:

• be labeled **C1, C2, C3, ...**
• describe a **fundamental or valuation-driven mechanism** affecting asset performance
• include at least one valid evidence citation
• explain why the mechanism affects expected returns or valuation

Claims should generally follow the structure:

**valuation signal → fundamental explanation → security implication**

Example structure:

C1 — Valuation dislocation
C2 — Earnings or profitability trajectory
C3 — Security-level mispricing thesis

Each claim should also include:

• Assumptions — conditions required for the claim to hold
• Falsifiers — what evidence would invalidate the claim

Example:

C1: A company trading at a valuation multiple significantly below historical norms despite stable earnings may experience multiple expansion if the market reassesses its earnings durability [AAPL-PE].

Assumptions:

* Earnings remain stable or improve
* No structural deterioration in the company’s competitive position

Falsifiers:

* Earnings decline materially
* Margins compress due to competitive pressure

Claims should focus primarily on **fundamental drivers of intrinsic value** rather than macro narratives.

---SECTION: portfolio_construction---

After stating claims, construct a portfolio allocation consistent with your reasoning.

For each position:

• Specify ticker and weight
• Explain which claim(s) justify the allocation
• Explain why the security appears mispriced relative to fundamentals
• Explain key downside risks

Portfolio weights should follow from the **strength and interaction of the claims**.

Example linkage:

If C1 and C2 identify a company whose earnings are improving while valuation remains depressed, overweight that security.

If a claim identifies excessive valuation relative to fundamentals, reduce exposure to that company.

Follow these rules carefully:

• Use **3–5 claims maximum**
• Every claim must cite evidence
• Every major allocation must reference at least one claim
• Avoid unsupported narrative speculation
• Distinguish clearly between evidence and interpretation

Your portfolio should clearly reflect **valuation-driven investment reasoning**.

---SECTION: reasoning_structure---

Your reasoning must follow this structure.

First, produce **numbered claims**.

Each claim must:

• be labeled **C1, C2, C3, ...**
• describe a **technical price or momentum mechanism** affecting asset performance
• include at least one valid evidence citation
• explain why the mechanism affects expected returns

Claims should generally follow the structure:

**price behavior → momentum implication → security positioning**

Example structure:

C1 — Trend regime
C2 — Momentum leadership
C3 — Security-level momentum thesis

Each claim should also include:

• Assumptions — conditions required for the claim to hold
• Falsifiers — what evidence would invalidate the claim

Example:

C1: Securities exhibiting strong medium-term momentum tend to outperform in the near term due to momentum persistence in equity markets [NVDA-MOM200].

Assumptions:

* Momentum persistence continues in the current market environment
* No major macro shock disrupts the trend structure

Falsifiers:

* Momentum metrics deteriorate rapidly
* Price breaks below key trend levels (e.g., major moving averages)

Claims should focus primarily on **observable price and momentum behavior**, not macro or fundamental narratives.

---SECTION: portfolio_construction---

After stating claims, construct a portfolio allocation consistent with your reasoning.

For each position:

• Specify ticker and weight
• Explain which claim(s) justify the allocation
• Explain the technical signals supporting the position
• Explain key downside risks

Portfolio weights should follow from the **strength and interaction of the claims**.

Example linkage:

If C1 identifies a strong momentum regime and C2 identifies securities with the strongest relative strength, overweight those securities.

If a claim identifies weakening momentum or trend breakdown risk, reduce exposure to those securities.

Follow these rules carefully:

• Use **3–5 claims maximum**
• Every claim must cite evidence
• Every major allocation must reference at least one claim
• Avoid unsupported narrative speculation
• Distinguish clearly between evidence and interpretation

Your portfolio should clearly reflect **technical momentum reasoning**.

---SECTION: reasoning_structure---

Your reasoning must follow this structure.

First, produce **numbered claims**.

Each claim must:

• be labeled **C1, C2, C3, ...**
• describe a **macro-driven causal mechanism** affecting asset performance
• include at least one valid evidence citation
• explain why the mechanism affects expected returns or sector performance

Claims should generally follow the structure:

**macro regime → sector or factor implication → security-level implication**

Example structure:

C1 — Monetary policy or interest-rate regime
C2 — Sector or factor implication from that regime
C3 — Security-level exposure benefiting from that regime

Each claim should also include:

• Assumptions — conditions required for the claim to hold
• Falsifiers — what evidence would invalidate the claim

Example:

C1: Rising real interest rates may compress valuation multiples for long-duration growth equities because higher discount rates reduce the present value of distant cash flows [L1-REAL].

Assumptions:

* Real rates remain elevated
* Equity risk premium does not expand enough to offset valuation compression

Falsifiers:

* Real yields fall materially
* Growth earnings acceleration offsets valuation pressure

When constructing claims, prioritize **macro causal mechanisms** rather than company-specific narratives.

Company-level reasoning should appear mainly in later claims.

---SECTION: portfolio_construction---

After stating claims, construct a portfolio allocation consistent with your reasoning.

For each position:

• Specify ticker and weight
• Explain which claim(s) justify the allocation
• Explain how the position benefits from the macro regime you identified

Your portfolio should clearly express a **macro thesis**.

Sector tilts and company selection should both reflect the macro regime implied by your claims.

Portfolio weights should follow from the **strength and interaction of the claims**.

Example linkage:

If C1 and C2 imply cyclical sector outperformance under economic expansion, overweight companies exposed to that cycle.

If a claim implies macro tightening or recession risk, tilt toward defensive sectors.

Follow these rules carefully:

• Use **3–5 claims maximum**
• Every claim must cite evidence
• Every major allocation must reference at least one claim
• Avoid unsupported narrative speculation
• Distinguish clearly between evidence and interpretation

Your portfolio should be **clearly consistent with the macro regime described in your claims**.

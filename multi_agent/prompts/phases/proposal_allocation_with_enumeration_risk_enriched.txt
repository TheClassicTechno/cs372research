---SECTION: reasoning_structure---

Your reasoning must follow this structure.

First, produce **numbered claims**.

Each claim must:

• be labeled **C1, C2, C3, ...**
• describe a **risk mechanism affecting portfolio stability**
• include at least one valid evidence citation
• explain why the mechanism affects portfolio risk or drawdown potential

Claims should generally follow the structure:

**risk driver → portfolio vulnerability → allocation adjustment**

Example structure:

C1 — Volatility regime implication
C2 — Correlation or concentration risk
C3 — Security-level risk contribution

Each claim should also include:

• Assumptions — conditions required for the claim to hold
• Falsifiers — what evidence would invalidate the claim

Example:

C1: Elevated market volatility increases portfolio drawdown risk, especially for high-beta securities, which may require smaller position sizes to maintain stable risk-adjusted returns [L1-VIX].

Assumptions:

* Volatility remains elevated
* Correlations increase during stress events

Falsifiers:

* Volatility compresses significantly
* Correlation structure weakens rather than strengthens

Claims should focus primarily on **portfolio risk dynamics** rather than expected returns alone.

---SECTION: portfolio_construction---

After stating claims, construct a portfolio allocation consistent with your reasoning.

For each position:

• Specify ticker and weight
• Explain which claim(s) justify the allocation
• Explain the position's contribution to portfolio risk
• Explain how the allocation affects diversification and drawdown exposure

Portfolio weights should follow from the **risk profile implied by the claims**.

Example linkage:

If C1 identifies an elevated volatility regime, reduce exposure to high-volatility securities.

If C2 identifies strong correlation clustering within a sector, reduce concentration in that sector.

If C3 identifies securities with favorable risk-adjusted characteristics, allocate modest exposure.

Follow these rules carefully:

• Use **3–5 claims maximum**
• Every claim must cite evidence
• Every major allocation must reference at least one claim
• Avoid unsupported narrative speculation
• Distinguish clearly between evidence and interpretation

Your portfolio should clearly reflect **risk-aware portfolio construction**.

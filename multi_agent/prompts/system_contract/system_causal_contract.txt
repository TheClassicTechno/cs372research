
## Causal Reasoning Contract (applies to ALL agents)

### Evidence Citation — MANDATORY
Every claim MUST cite data using the exact bracketed evidence IDs from the memo.
Valid IDs use the format [TICKER-METRIC] or [L1-METRIC], e.g. [AAPL-RET60], [L1-VIX],
[NVDA-F3], [TSLA-BETA]. These IDs appear verbatim in the memo — find and copy them.
NEVER invent your own labels. NEVER use free-text slugs like "aaplrevenuegrowth",
"fedinterestrates", or "portfoliorisk" — these are INVALID and will be scored as zero.
If you cannot find a bracketed ID for a claim, do not make that claim.

### Claim Classification
Every claim MUST include a reasoning_type:
- "causal": if X changes, Y will follow (intervention or counterfactual reasoning)
- "observational": X is associated with Y (correlation or pattern)
- "risk_assessment": X creates vulnerability to Y (conditional risk)
- "pattern": price behavior suggests continuation or reversal

You MUST include at least one causal or risk_assessment claim.
Pure observational associations are insufficient for trading decisions.

For each claim, explicitly state:
  - Your assumptions (what must hold for this claim to be valid)
  - What would falsify this claim

### Mandatory Uncertainty Disclosure
You MUST include a "risks_or_falsifiers" field answering:
1. What specific evidence or event would CHANGE YOUR MIND?
2. What is the probability you are wrong? (not 0%)
3. What confounder could explain this pattern without your proposed mechanism?

### Causal Reasoning Traps to Avoid
- Reverse causation: Does Y actually cause X instead?
- Confounding: Is there a Z that causes both X and Y?
- Selection bias: Are you only seeing data that confirms your view?
- Post-hoc narrative: Are you fitting a story to past data?
- Survivorship bias: What failures are you not seeing?

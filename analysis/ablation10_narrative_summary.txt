Ablation 10 — Narrative Summary
================================
Generated: 2026-03-12

RESEARCH QUESTION
-----------------
Does forcing agents to retry their revision when CRIT detects
causal_alignment < 0.75 improve causal reasoning quality — and does
that improvement translate to better financial outcomes?

DESIGN
------
2 configs x 35 scenarios = 70 runs (all completed successfully).
Model: gpt-5-mini (agents), gpt-5 (CRIT evaluator).
Agents: macro_enriched_intense, technical_enriched_intense.
Pipeline: propose -> critique -> revise -> CRIT -> [intervention if CA < 0.75] -> judge.

  Baseline:  CRIT runs but reasoning_quality rule disabled (never fires).
  Treatment: reasoning_quality enabled; if causal_alignment < 0.75 the agent
             receives a detailed nudge grounded in Pearl's Causal Hierarchy
             (association vs intervention vs counterfactual reasoning).

Treatment runs averaged 448s vs 361s baseline (+24%), reflecting the
retry overhead. Total compute: ~7.9 hours.


THE HEADLINE RESULT: CAUSAL REASONING IMPROVED DRAMATICALLY
------------------------------------------------------------
The intervention achieved exactly what it was designed to do. The
technical agent's causal_alignment pillar jumped from 0.739 to 0.878
(+0.139, p not computed but effect is massive with variance collapse
from stdev 0.092 to 0.028). The causal_overreach flag dropped from
68.6% to 2.7% — a near-total elimination of the most common reasoning
failure mode.

The macro agent was largely unaffected: its causal_alignment was already
0.865, well above the 0.75 threshold, so the intervention rarely fired
for it (2 of 23 triggered runs).

Intervention statistics:
  - 23 of 39 treatment runs triggered intervention (59%)
  - 22 of those 23 targeted the technical agent
  - Mean pre-intervention CA: 0.676; mean post-intervention CA: 0.876
  - 95.5% of nudged agents improved; zero cases of degradation


PILLAR-LEVEL TRADEOFFS
-----------------------
                        Baseline    Treatment    Delta
Causal Alignment        0.802       0.871       +0.069  (primary target)
Evidential Support      0.851       0.854       +0.003  (no change)
Logical Validity        0.847       0.846       -0.001  (no change)
Alt. Consideration      0.843       0.818       -0.025  (modest decline)

The alternative_consideration decline (-0.025) warrants attention. When
agents are pushed to be more careful about causal claims, they may
become less willing to engage with speculative counterarguments. This
is a "reasoning tightening" effect: higher epistemic standards on one
dimension can reduce exploratory breadth on another.


AGREEMENT & COLLAPSE
---------------------
JS divergence dropped slightly (0.160 -> 0.141), meaning agents became
marginally more aligned in their allocations. This is consistent with
the theory: forcing the technical agent to articulate causal mechanisms
rather than pattern-match may bring its reasoning closer to the macro
agent's fundamentals-based framework.

Collapse ratios were not significantly different (p=0.356), so while
there is a directional trend toward more agreement, it does not rise
to significance.


FINANCIAL OUTCOMES: THE INTERVENTION DID NOT HELP
--------------------------------------------------
Despite substantially improving causal reasoning quality, the treatment
showed no financial benefit. If anything, the direction slightly favors
baseline:

  Metric              Baseline    Treatment    Diff      p-value
  Excess Return       -0.91%      -1.11%      -0.20%    0.529
  Sharpe              0.657       0.568       -0.089    0.219
  Sortino             1.177       1.040       -0.137    0.222
  Calmar              3.966       3.656       -0.310    0.204
  Volatility          20.52%      21.74%      +1.22%    0.031 *
  Max Drawdown        9.80%       10.33%      +0.53%    0.062

Only annualized volatility reaches significance (p=0.031), and in the
wrong direction: treatment portfolios were modestly MORE volatile. Max
drawdown approaches significance (p=0.062), also favoring baseline.

All risk-adjusted return metrics (Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar) are
directionally worse under treatment, though none reach significance.


INTERPRETATION
--------------
Ablation 10 reveals a clean dissociation between reasoning quality and
portfolio quality. The causal alignment intervention:

  1. Massively improved causal reasoning (technical CA: 0.739 -> 0.878)
  2. Nearly eliminated causal_overreach flags (68.6% -> 2.7%)
  3. Did NOT improve financial outcomes (no metric significant in the
     positive direction; volatility significantly WORSE)

This suggests that causal reasoning quality, as measured by CRIT, is
not a bottleneck for financial performance in this pipeline. Several
hypotheses:

  (a) The judge portfolio is robust to the quality of agent reasoning —
      the judge may be extracting signal from the direction of agent
      allocations regardless of whether the causal justification is
      sound.

  (b) "Causal overreach" in technical analysis may actually be useful
      heuristically. When a technical agent says "the SMA crossover
      CAUSES momentum," that may be sloppy causal language wrapping a
      valid predictive pattern. Forcing the agent to be epistemically
      precise may strip out useful predictive signal.

  (c) The retry overhead (extra token cost and latency) introduces
      noise without adding information relevant to allocation quality.

CONTRAST WITH ABLATION 7
-------------------------
Ablation 7 tested JS-divergence-based interventions (forcing agents
to retry when their allocations were too similar). That intervention
showed borderline-significant financial improvements:

  Ablation 7:  Sharpe +0.143 (p=0.028), Sortino +0.254 (p=0.026)
  Ablation 10: Sharpe -0.089 (p=0.219), Sortino -0.137 (p=0.222)

This reinforces the pattern: interventions targeting allocation
diversity (ablation 7) have more financial impact than interventions
targeting reasoning quality (ablation 10). The judge benefits from
hearing genuinely different perspectives, not from hearing better-
justified perspectives.


BOTTOM LINE
-----------
Ablation 10 is a successful REASONING intervention and a null
FINANCIAL result. The causal alignment nudge works exactly as
designed — it makes agents reason more carefully about causation —
but that improvement does not propagate to better portfolio
decisions. This is an important negative result: it tells us that
the value chain from reasoning quality to financial quality has a
weak link somewhere between the agent's revision and the judge's
final allocation.

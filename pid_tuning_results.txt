Key Observations
----------------
1. Quality is generally high -- rho_bar ranges from 0.75 to 0.875 (target is 0.8).
   Most cases land in CONVERGED quadrant, so beta decays (0.5 -> 0.45) as expected.

2. Full PID caught a STUCK case -- the derivative term (Kd=0.03) made the controller
   more sensitive, and case 1 with rho_bar=0.750 was classified STUCK. Beta correctly
   bumped from 0.5 -> 0.6 (more adversarial). This is the PID doing its job.

3. High Kp triggered extra round -- case 2 ran 2 rounds instead of 1 because the
   stronger Kp produced a larger correction signal. Beta decayed further (0.45 -> 0.405)
   in round 2.

4. Ki barely contributes -- with only 1-2 rounds, the integral term is tiny
   (I-term ~0.0001-0.0005). Ki would matter more with longer debates (more rounds
   to accumulate).

5. Causal integrity is the weakest pillar -- consistently scores 0.5 across all
   experiments. This is a prompt/model issue, not a PID issue.

6. Original Ki=0.03 was correctly rejected -- the stability check (Eq 17) caught
   that Ki=0.03 with T_max=20 would violate the stability bound (1.35 > 1.11).
   We lowered to Ki=0.01 which passes (0.63 < 1.11).

Recommendation for Veljko
-------------------------
For the current 3-round setup, Full PID (Kp=0.15, Ki=0.01, Kd=0.03) is the most
interesting -- it's the only config that detected a STUCK case and bumped beta.
If you increase max_rounds to 5+, the Ki term will become more impactful. The
high Kp (0.4) is also worth exploring for faster reactions, but watch for
overshoot in longer debates.

EXPERIMENT 6: 5 Rounds (test Ki accumulation)
================================================================================
Date: 2026-03-03
Setup: Full PID (Kp=0.15, Ki=0.01, Kd=0.03), NVDA Q1, max 5 rounds

Case     | PID rounds | Rd1 rho_bar | Quadrant  | beta      | u_t
NVDA     | 1          | 0.812       | CONVERGED | 0.5->0.45 | -0.0024
NVDA_bk  | 1          | 0.875       | CONVERGED | 0.5->0.45 | -0.0142

Observations:
10. Early termination works correctly -- despite max_rounds=5, both cases
    converged after round 1 (JS divergence near 0). The PID's termination
    check prevented unnecessary extra rounds.

11. Ki still minimal at 1 round -- with only 1 PID step, the integral term
    stays at ~0.0001-0.0007. To truly test Ki accumulation, we'd need cases
    where quality stays persistently low (STUCK for 3+ consecutive rounds).

12. The current NVDA data is too "easy" -- agents consistently score 0.75-0.875
    on CRIT, which is near or above the target (0.8). To stress-test Ki, we need
    harder cases or lower rho_star to force more PID rounds.

Next Steps for Ki Testing:
- Lower pid_rho_star to 0.9 (harder target) to keep PID active longer
- Use more contentious tickers or deliberately adversarial case data
- Or increase initial_beta to 0.8 to start in a more adversarial regime
